# Process Overview
This is the initial customer contact process where inquiries are received, requirements are gathered, and formal quotes are prepared. This process establishes the foundation for the customer relationship and ensures all parties understand scope, pricing, and expectations before orders are placed.

## Responsibilities
- **Primary Department**: Sales
- **Supporting Roles**: Sales Engineering, Estimating, Pricing
- **Key Stakeholders**: Customer, Engineering (for technical feasibility), Finance (for pricing approval)

## Inputs
What this process receives to begin work:
- Customer inquiry (email, phone, RFQ, website form)
- Technical requirements or specifications
- Quantity requirements
- Delivery timeline expectations
- Budget constraints (if provided)

## Process Steps
1. **Receive Inquiry**: Log customer contact and requirements in CRM system
2. **Technical Review**: Assess feasibility and identify any technical challenges
3. **Cost Estimation**: Calculate material, labor, and overhead costs
4. **Prepare Quote**: Generate formal quotation with pricing, terms, and lead time
5. **Internal Review**: Route quote for management approval if needed
6. **Submit to Customer**: Send formal quote to customer with terms and conditions
7. **Follow Up**: Track quote status and respond to customer questions

## Outputs
What this process produces for the next stage:
- Formal quote document with pricing and terms
- Quote logged in CRM system
- Technical feasibility assessment documented
- Customer acceptance (verbal or written) when quote is approved
- Approved quote ready for order entry (Process B)

## Tools & Systems
- CRM system for customer tracking
- Quoting software or ERP estimating module
- Product catalog and pricing database
- Email and document management system

## Notes
- Quotes over $100K require VP Sales approval before submission
- Standard quote validity is 30 days unless otherwise specified
- Technical products may require engineering review before quoting
- International customers require export compliance screening
- Always confirm delivery lead time with Planning before committing to customer
